RESERACH PAPERS

Contextual Bias Score : A Quantitative Metric for Gender Bias Detection in Visual Datasets

This paper quantify gender bias in images by analyzing the influence of surrounding objects and scene contexts on gender representation. Using object detectors (YOLOv8), segmentation models (SAM), and the CLIP model with Places365, the study generates interpretable bias heatmaps. SHAP and XGBoost are used to provide feature importance and bias scoring, highlighting which visual elements reinforce stereotypical gender roles.

Download the paper

Audio Bias Score: Bias Detection Metric for Gender Bias Detection in Audio Datasets

This paper propose a modality-specific bias metric for audio by analyzing raw voice features (pitch, energy, amplitude, ZCR, etc.) before any training occurs. It would use symbolic and polynomial regression to detect consistent feature differences across genders. The paper would also validate bias scores through controlled data augmentation, resulting in a scaled score (e.g., +0.75 indicating 75% male bias). This work addresses bias intrinsic to the dataset, independent of model behavior.

Download the paper

Text Modality

This paper present a method for detecting gender bias in pre-trained language models (like BERT) at the sentence level. It would explore how gendered terms influence sentence embeddings using cosine similarity, Pointwise Mutual Information (PMI), and contextual shifts. The analysis uses the WinoBias dataset, with tools such as PCA, SHAP, and Random Forest to interpret the bias and provide a bias score per occupation. The study would also discuss how such bias affects downstream NLP tasks.

Download the paper

Video Modality

This study focus on gender bias in video-based human action recognition datasets. It would introduce a composite bias score built from three dimensions: embedding disparities between genders, visual salience bias (which part of the video draws focus), and representation imbalance across classes. The paper would include visualizations and action-level fairness indicators to interpret how framing and dataset composition reinforce gender bias.

Download the paper

RESAI TOOOLKIT:Unified Framework for Multimodal Gender Bias Detection

This paper presents a comprehensive framework integrating gender bias detection across multiple data modalities—text, image, audio, and video. It consolidates individual modality-specific metrics into a unified toolkit (the RESAI Toolkit) to provide interpretable, explainable bias scores. The study emphasizes the importance of addressing bias at the dataset level, independent of downstream models, and validates the approach through statistical tests and explainability methods. The group paper also discusses the modular design that allows easy extension to other types of biases and evolving AI models, demonstrating the framework’s scalability and practical relevance for fair AI research.
